HOW TO USE MAPFORGE v4

This is the map of the whole game world — every edit saves instantly into game/data/usmap.json, the exact file the game loads. The amber flash bottom-right confirms each write. Restart the game and your world is live.

Moving around

Wheel zooms at the mouse. Middle-drag (or hold SPACE and drag) pans. FIT shows the whole country; MERID. jumps to the starting town. The minimap (top right) shows where you are — click it to jump.

The workflow story

  1. Arrange the highways — the interstates are the spine (ROAD tool).
  2. Drop EXITS along them — each one numbers itself by milepost, the same law the game signs use.
  3. Draw DISTRICTS and pin NOTES — plan the places before you build them.
  4. MEASURE drives — check the distances feel right before committing.
  5. Place structures at the exits (PLACE + the catalog).

The tools

SELECT — click anything: roads, exits, towns, placements, districts, notes. The Inspector (right) shows its row. Selected roads grow vertex handles — drag them to reshape; changes save on release and the junction bake re-runs by itself. Drag an exit's diamond to slide it along its highway (it renumbers by milepost — that's the law).

Click open land to select its STATE — the region lights up and the Inspector becomes the state card: its ruler (rulers.json is live — attitude multiplies your infamy for crimes committed there), bandit strength, towns (click to jump), exits, districts, biome mix, and 🐾 what lives there. ZOOM TO STATE frames it.

The ECOLOGY layer paints wildlife richness over the land (greener = more creature rows can live there — the swamp glows; that's knifeback country). With it on, the footer's hover line lists exactly which creatures the cell under your mouse can spawn.

Placements are true footprints — zoom in and every placed building shows its real catalog footprint (rotated with its rot; the pale notch is the door edge), so you can lay a main street without overlapping walls.

ROAD — pick or NEW a road, then each click drops the next point on the nearest end (so you can stretch a highway from either side). Double-click a segment inserts a point mid-road. Right-click a vertex deletes it. Every point edit preserves the road's character — nickname, danger, toll, lanes, surface all survive.

EXIT — click where the exit should LEAD. Pick a piece: exit only, full interchange, or a combo that also stamps a waystation/hamlet/outpost at the destination. The node anchors on the nearest interstate, numbers itself by milepost (MERIDIAN = I-95 EXIT 9 is the canon anchor), and the game builds the ramps, gore paint and the big green sign from the row.

MEASURE — click A, then B. You get: straight-line distance, the routed distance along real roads, minutes at the wheel and the game-clock hours (60×), for the vehicle picked on the right. This is "how long would it take to drive there" answered before you build.

DISTRICT — click polygon corners, double-click or ENTER to close, name it (downtown / port / combat zone...). Districts save into the map file — future territory systems read them.

NOTE — pin a shared TODO on the map (open → doing → done). This is the collaborative plan layer: your notes and the AI's notes live in the same file, so both of you see what's planned where.

PAINT / TOWN / PLACE — as always: biomes, town founding, pinning catalog structures.

Network health

The right panel counts the road network's orphans — street grids and spurs the junction bake never tied to the trunk (why a GPS route can't reach them). Toggle the ORPHANS layer to paint them red, then fix them by dragging their feeder road to actually touch, and BAKE.

Housekeeping

BAKE re-runs the junction law by hand (it auto-runs ~2s after road edits). UNDO/REDO (Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Y) cover every edit. RELOAD re-reads all files from disk.

Keys: 1/3/5/9 brush · SPACE+drag pan · Esc cancels dialogs/tools · Ctrl+Z/Y undo/redo · Del deletes the selection.
