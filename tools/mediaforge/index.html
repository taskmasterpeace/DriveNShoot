HOW TO USE MEDIAFORGE

MediaForge puts real videos and music into the game. Films play on the safehouse TV (walk up to it, press E) and at the drive-in; folders of mp3s become radio stations and the game's music. Everything here writes straight into the game's own files — there is no export step.

Films & clips — the whole pipeline

  1. Drop an MP4 (or .mov / .mkv / .webm) into game/media/film — or tvshow / trailers / clips, whichever shelf fits.
  2. It appears under INGEST on the right. (The page re-checks every 15 seconds, and after every job.)
  3. Check the little category dropdown next to it, then press ⚙ CONVERT → .OGV.
  4. Done — it joins the library on the left, and it's on the in-game TV and the drive-in.

Posters and runtime are automatic. Converting turns your file into .ogv — the only video the game engine plays — grabs a poster frame, and measures the length. You never make thumbnails by hand. Watch the Job log (bottom right) for progress; long films take a while and the buttons grey out until the job finishes.

The library (left side)

Every converted video is one row. Edit anything in place — it saves by itself (watch for the amber flash bottom-right):

  • The title — click the big name and type.
  • The unlock dropdown — who gets to watch it:
    • always_available — on TV from the start.
    • found_dvd / found_tape / found_reel — the film becomes a collectible: a physical pickup at the drive-in that the player must find in the world before it plays.
    • quest_reward — unlocked by finishing a quest.
    • regional_channel — only airs in one region; fill in region.
    • world_event — appears when a world event fires.
  • screens — where it may play: safehouse_tv, drive_in, public_tv, news (comma-separated).
  • pri — 0–9; higher plays earlier in the TV rotation.
  • pack — only appears if the optional media pack is installed.
  • preview ▸ — watch the converted file in your browser.
  • ✕ — removes the row only; the files stay on disk, so it's safe.

The test buttons (no files needed)

▚ GENERATE TEST CLIP conjures a 12-second test pattern with tone out of thin air and runs it through the whole pipeline — the fastest way to prove the in-game TV works before you have real films. ♪ GENERATE TEST MUSIC writes two synthetic music beds into the music folders to prove the radio path the same way.

Radio stations — a folder of mp3s = a station

  1. Type a name (e.g. Chicago Radio) and press + CREATE — the station's folder is made for you.
  2. Drop mp3s into that folder. That's it — the station is on the dial.

In the car: O radio power · L next station · , / . volume.

Music folders

game/media/music/radio and game/media/music/game take plain mp3s — no conversion, no rows. radio feeds the radio's music stations; game is the game's own soundtrack.

The footer counts your rows; they live in game/data/media_manifest.json. Esc closes this guide. Hover any button or field for a one-line reminder.
