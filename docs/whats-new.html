v0.11.0 2026-07-06 The Remote Eye & The Fun Pass

The world changed while you were gone? Send the bird before you send yourself — and bring a friend.

  • New

    The scout drone flies your route while you stay home.

    A dock by the safehouse door launches it along your map course. It marks hazards on your map, comes home to charge — and it can be shot down and lost out there.

  • New

    Co-op that keeps you together.

    Name tags over your partner, an arrow that always points to them, respawn beside them instead of across the map, a bed rig parked and waiting, and horns that carry over the wire.

  • New

    PvP with rules you can read.

    Peace, duel, or free-for-all — one key cycles it. The safehouse yard is holy ground, kills post a bounty everyone can see on your tag, and your machine decides what hurts you.

  • Improved

    Every walk, wag, and leap is now live-tunable data.

    The animator's numbers moved into rows the MotionForge editor drives — tweak in the browser, reload in-game, watch the stride change.

v0.10.0 2026-07-06 The Cinema Update

The wasteland got screens: a TV in your safehouse, a drive-in off the highway, and a world that broadcasts its own news.

  • New

    The safehouse TV.

    Press E and watch your own film catalog inside the game. Time passes while a reel rolls — downtime costs daylight, on purpose.

  • New

    The drive-in theater.

    A glowing screen off the Meridian road. Fire the projector: trailers first, then the feature. Drive off mid-show and it stops for you.

  • New

    Films as loot.

    DVDs, tapes, and reels lie in the world — take one and that exact film unlocks on your shelf, forever. New films scatter their own pickups automatically.

  • New

    Public screens with channels.

    Bar TVs run loops nobody chose. When a faction takes the state, the screen retunes to their feed — and breaking news cuts in.

  • New

    The world reports itself.

    A state falls → the TV says so. A bounty lands on your head → the radio says so. Two channels, two screens, one world.

  • Improved

    The radio and TV each drain their own news.

    The dial can't eat the television's bulletins anymore — each screen airs its own.

v0.9.0 2026-07-06 The Moveset + The Media Forges

Your body learned to fight bare-handed, your dog learned to jump, and the game grew a film-studio pipeline.

  • New

    Hold CTRL to crouch — one new key, a whole stealth layer.

    You read smaller and quieter, fit under low gaps, and tapping it at a sprint converts your speed into a slide.

  • New

    Empty hands are a weapon.

    Tap to punch a real combo, hold to shove space open, strike at a sprint to tackle someone flat — and the new Martial Arts skill unlocks kicks, throws, and ground finishers as you use it.

  • New

    Drag bodies and crates where you need them.

    Hold E to haul — slow, heavy, and it builds Strength.

  • New

    Water is real terrain.

    Wade the shallows, swim the open — it drains your lungs and takes your hands off your weapons. Empty lungs and the water starts taking you.

  • New

    Your dogs got their verbs.

    They leap fences to stay at your heel, attacks become flying pounces, and Hunters smell buried caches and dig them up.

  • New

    MotionForge — animation as sliders and sentences.

    Every gait in the game is editable numbers in a web tool — or just type "make the sniff deeper and slower."

  • New

    MediaForge — drop MP4s, get in-game media.

    One click converts your films to the engine's format with a poster and runtime, ready for the TV and the drive-in. MP3 folders feed the radio.

  • Improved

    The whole movement kit reads as one body.

    Sprint, combat stance, the dive, and the new low stance all respect each other.

  • Fixed

    Dogs no longer panic over vanished targets.

    An attack order on a target that died mid-chase used to spam errors every frame. It doesn't anymore.
