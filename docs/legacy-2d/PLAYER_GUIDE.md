# CARWORLD: THE LONG HAUL - PLAYER GUIDE

Welcome to the wastes. Your goal is simple: Drive as far as you can, scavenge what you find, and get back alive.

## 🎮 CONTROLS

## 🚘 THE CORE LOOP

1.  **DRIVE**: Navigate the infinite highway. Avoid **Wrecks** (static obstacles). Distance = Score.
2.  **MAINTAIN**: Your car will take damage and eventually **Break Down**.
    *   **Breakdowns**: You lose power. Stop, get out, and **Hold E** to repair.
    *   **Cost**: Repairing costs **1 Repair Kit**. You start with 1.
3.  **SCAVENGE**: Look for **Loot Caches** (piles of tires/scrap) on the roadside.
    *   **Hold E** to scavenge.
    *   **Rewards**: Repair Kits (Crucial) or **Scrap** (Score/Currency).
4.  **SURVIVE**: Managing **Heat** is the key to survival.
5.  **EXTRACT**: Bank your run.
    *   **Minimum Distance**: You must drive at least **0.3 miles** to bank anything.
    *   If you die or total your car, you lose everything.

## 🔥 THE HEAT SYSTEM

Everything you do generates **Heat**. Heat attracts **Pursuers**.

*   **Driving**: Generates low heat over time.
*   **Crashing**: +5 Heat.
*   **Looting**: +10 Heat.
*   **Repairing**: +15 Heat (Noise attracts attention).

### Threat Levels
*   **LOW (<25)**: You are safe.
*   **MED (25-39)**: **Pursuer Risk**. An enemy might spawn if you linger.
*   **HIGH (40+)**: **GUARANTEED PURSUER**. A Red Interceptor will hunt you down.

**The Pursuer**: A fast, aggressive enemy vehicle.
*   It spawns behind you.
*   It tries to **RAM** you off the road.
*   **Counter-play**: Drive fast (>120mph) to delay its spawn. Trick it into crashing into Wrecks.

---

## 💡 SURVIVAL TIPS

1.  **Don't Stop Unless You Must**: Stopping allows Heat to catch up and Pursuers to spawn.
2.  **Loot Strategically**: If your Heat is near 25, looting that cache might trigger a fight. Is it worth the Kit?
3.  **Clean Driving**: Crashing into wrecks damages your car AND raises heat. Avoid damage to stay under the radar.
4.  **Greed Kills**: If you have 0 Kits and high HP damage, **Extract**. Don't push for that next mile.
