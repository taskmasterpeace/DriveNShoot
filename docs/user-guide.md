# DRIVN — Player's Guide

**Current as of v0.13.0** · updated 2026-07-07
> See [what's new](SHIPPING-LOG.md) for changes since the last version.

Top-down vehicular survival across a compressed United States. This is the **player's**
guide — the same seven manuals that sit on the bookshelf inside your safehouse
(walk up, press **E — 📚 read the MANUALS**). Every key named here is the *default*;
**F11** rebinds anything, keyboard and pad both.

[SCREENSHOT: library-shelf]

## Quick Start — your first ten minutes

1. **PLAY.bat** → the DRIVN title → **NEW GAME**. THE FIRST RUN guides you: drive,
   pull over, scavenge, get home. Do what the single HUD line says.
2. You start near **Meridian** and the two-story **safehouse** — it's yours. The supply
   chest inside has food, meds, a shotgun, gadgets, and *Manual: Home & Survival*.
3. Take the car (**E** at the door), drive the interstate (**W/A/S/D**, **SPACE**
   handbrake), and follow the CIRCUIT pips on the HUD: scavenge → upgrade → push → node.
4. **F5** saves. If you go down, you wake at the safehouse — lighter. Your dogs are the
   only permadeath in this game. Protect them.

## Who this is for

One player (or a co-op partner — **F7** host / **F8** join, PvP rules on **F6**).
No roles, no accounts: your save file is the world.

## The Seven Manuals

The full instruction set lives **in the game** as books — on the safehouse shelf and as
findable items you read from the pack (**TAB**, then USE). The same content, chapter by
chapter:

### 🚗 Driver's Handbook
**E** enters any rig. **W** gas · **S** brake/reverse · **A/D** steer · **SPACE** handbrake
(at speed: a controlled drift; the yaw is capped so you never snap a 180). Standing on the
gas from a stop lights a **burnout**. Every vehicle has **five parts you feel failing** —
engine (misfires), tires (slop), battery (flicker), fuel tank (leak), chassis — repaired
with salvaged car parts / tire kits. Jerry cans pour +40 fuel. Dirt is slower than asphalt
and your **tires** decide by how much. In heavy states (Colorado…) the far wilderness
genuinely **rolls** — you feel grades through the suspension. Race boards run checkpoint
races against your own ghost. [SCREENSHOT: driving-drift]

### 🏃 On Foot: The Moveset
**SHIFT** sprint · **CTRL** hold-crouch (×0.55 noise; sprint+tap = **slide**) · **SPACE**
dive. Water: wade → swim → drown if stamina dies. **LMB** tap = punch (jab-jab-cross
combo), hold = shove, sprint+tap = tackle. **Martial Arts** levels by doing: lv2 KICKS,
lv4 THROWS, lv6 FINISHERS — watch them light up in the skill tree (**U**). **`** cycles
weapons, **R** reloads, **G** grenade, **B** binoculars. Kills leave **bodies you loot
with E** (no crates); bodies decay, picked-clean ones faster. Cars kill pedestrians —
including you. [SCREENSHOT: unarmed-combo]

### 🐕 The Pack: Dogs
**E** adopts a stray, **P** pets, feed with meat. Bond: STRAY → COMPANION → PARTNER →
SOULBOUND (the prompt wears the tier). **C whistles**: ×1 heel · ×2 guard · ×3 seek ·
hold SIC · ×4 SHIELD (SOULBOUND only). Dogs jump fences, pounce on SIC, Hunters dig
buried caches. **THE DOG'S EYE**: a PARTNER+ dog sent seeking carries a split-screen view —
the tighter the bond, the farther it ranges before the screen splits. Dogs are **mortal**:
bandage a downed dog in time, or dig a grave and keep the collar. [SCREENSHOT: dog-eye-split]

### 🏠 Home & Survival
The safehouse: supply chest, corner **TV** (E to watch, arrows = channels, slider =
volume), **drone dock** and **build board** by the door (scrap → walls I–III, garage,
kennel, workbench, bed), kennel across the street. A day is 24 real minutes; the **moon**
sets how dark night gets; **T** waits. **Hunger** drains on the clock — eat. Wounds tax
six body parts (limp, shaky aim); bandage the worst or medkit everything. **F5/F9**
save/load. Going down = wake at home, minus a cut of scrap/scrip; the rig stays where it
fell. **K** = the world sheet · **U** = skill tree · **M** = map · **F** = home beacon.

### 🛸 Gadgets & Signals
**Drone**: USE deploys a patrol scout; USE again to **take the stick** — your body stands
still and the **screen splits** as you fly (**WASD**). You can't power off mid-air: **E**
lands it; if you're attacked it hovers and you snap back. Docks recharge in a **quarter of
a day** (T-wait counts). The military **Humvee** carries a drone bay on its deck.
**Surveillance camera**: plant it; **V** cycles the corner window to its feed. **Motion
sensor**: pings from where it stands. **Walkie-talkie**: key it — movement bleeds through
the static with a compass direction. **O** opens the **radio dial** — drag across the FM
band, stations lock, static hisses between; **Y** scans for distress calls.
[SCREENSHOT: radio-dial]

### 🎠 The Carousel
Ten buried gates. Approach one and its **occupiers wake**. The terminal names the price:
**purge** / **launch codes** (earned or bought) / **power** (jerry cans, power cells).
Then the **spin-up** — loud, waves come — hold the room until the ring burns teal. An
active gate grows a **portal**: activate it and the computer counts you down — *ten, nine,
eight…* — then **THE DIAL rolls** and you're across the country. Lit nodes get besieged;
relieve them or they go dark. Cheyenne's **targeting core** makes the jump follow your
map course. [SCREENSHOT: carousel-portal]

### 🪧 The Divided States
The USA at 60:1 — hours of highway in minutes. Every state has a **ruler** who reads your
ledger at the border: welcomes or bounty hunters. **Respect** is per-faction/state and
moves prices and standing. THE CIRCUIT (scavenge → upgrade → push → node) is the loop;
radio (**Y**) feeds it. Night is dangerous *because you can't see* — howlers hunt in
role-based packs (circlers, chargers, a SCREAMER that calls more), lurkers wait indoors.
Headlights and flares scare what the dark sends.

## Troubleshooting

- **"I hit a button and another game loaded"** — fixed for good: the legacy 2D game is
  quarantined outside the project. If a menu swallows input, Esc backs out of any panel.
- **The drone won't relaunch** — the dock charges a quarter of a day. The prompt shows
  the %; **T**-waiting speeds the clock and the charge with it.
- **"My dog won't kick down a door"** — dogs jump and dig, doors are yours.
- **Night is too dark** — that's the moon's call (8-day cycle). Headlights, flares, or wait.
- **A manual is missing from the shelf** — all seven also exist as pack items; the
  starter chest has *Home & Survival*.
