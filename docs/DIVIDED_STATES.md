# THE DIVIDED STATES OF AMERICA — Lore Bible

**Last Updated:** 2026-07-06 · **Status:** canon reference. Keep it terse — this is a table, not a novel.

---

## Premise

The Union broke. No single collapse — a cascade: grid failure, a hard fracture of the federal
government, then the states cut loose and never came back. Fifty polities now, each ruled its own
way by whoever held the water, the guns, or the last working factory. Borders are real again —
welcome signs, tolls, checkpoints. There is no capital. There is no president of anything but
Texas (and he says otherwise). You drive between them for a living. This is the **Divided States
of America** — the Old Union is a rumor your grandfather told you, and the interstates that
connected it are now the most dangerous places on Earth.

## THE CAROUSEL

Before the fracture, the federal government ran a **Continuity-of-Government (COG)** program:
a classified network of **instantaneous transit gates** built into hardened military bases, so
the chain of command could reconstitute anywhere in the country within seconds of a decapitation
strike. They called the network **THE CAROUSEL** — you step onto the platform in one base, the
ring spins up, and you step off in another, a thousand miles away, between one breath and the
next. It was never meant for civilians. It was meant to move Presidents and warheads.

The government it was built to save is gone. **The Carousel is not.** It runs on sealed geothermal
and buried reactor cores that were engineered to outlive the men who poured the concrete, and it
still faithfully links the old base network coast to coast. A **Carousel Station** is a real place
you can walk into: a blast-door vault under a ruined fort, a circular chamber ringed with dead
status boards, a slotted **ring platform** that hums awake when a valid **access key** is inserted.
The air tastes of ozone and cold metal. Stepping on feels like a held breath and a long fall that
ends instantly — most travelers vomit their first time; some never stop bleeding from the ears.

Access is the whole game. The rings only answer to **COG access keys** — stamped tokens, code
cylinders, or biometric fobs looted from the dead of the old government, traded between states,
or hoarded by the powers that grasped what the network is worth. Whoever holds the keys to a
region's stations controls fast travel, contraband routes, and troop movement across a broken
continent — which is why **Barons garrison their stations, and warlords kill for the codes.**
The risk is never zero: a **misjump** (bad code, damaged ring, or a station someone sabotaged on
the far end) can strand you in a sealed vault, drop you into hostile territory, or scatter you as
a smear across two coordinates. You use the Carousel because the drive would take a week and the
roads would kill you first — but a bullet is honest, and the Carousel is not.

> **The Carousel fully replaces the Deathlands "Redoubt" (hidden military bunker) and
> "MAT-TRANS" (matter-transfer chamber) concepts.** A *Carousel Station* is our Redoubt;
> *the Carousel* is our MAT-TRANS. Do not use the old terms.

## Renames — Deathlands → Ours

| Old term (Deathlands) | Our term | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Jack** (currency) | **Scrip** | Emergency stamped/printed money is what a broken government actually leaves behind; distinctive, real-world, not Deathlands vocabulary. |
| **Ville** (small town) | **Holdout** | A settlement that *held out* through the fracture; evocative and native to our premise. |
| **Barony / Baron** | *kept, but generalized* | "Baron" is a common post-apoc term, not Deathlands-exclusive — but in the Divided States rulers **vary** (King/President/CEO/Chief/Council/Warlord). Use the state's actual ruler-type; reserve "Baron" for states that genuinely have one. |
| **Redoubt** (hidden mil. bunker) | **Carousel Station** | Folded into the teleport network — the station *is* the old Redoubt, now a fast-travel node. |
| **MAT-TRANS** (matter transfer) | **The Carousel** | Our named teleport network + the act of jumping. |
| **Pre-dark** (before the apocalypse) | **Pre-Fracture** / **the Old Union** | "The dark" was Deathlands' nuke event; ours is a fracture/cascade, so the adjective changes with it. |
| **Sec-man / Sec-boss** | *kept (generic)* | Generic "security" contraction, not Deathlands-exclusive; safe to keep. |
